Part II - Data Expo 2009: Airline on time data¶

by Joseph Abang¶

Investigation Overview¶

The analysis' findings are divided into three categories: flight information(counts),flight information(delays) and carrier information. Each insight begins with a response to a question that touches on all of these categories, followed by the sharing of two additional insightful ideas that don't fall into any of the categories. A few simple questions, some of which are listed below, yielded all of the important insights.

  • Are there more flights on weekends?
  • How consistent are daily flight totals?
  • When is the best day of the week to fly and arrive on time?
  • When are the most late arrivals and departures at airports?
  • Which airline is best known for its short, medium, and long-distance flights?
  • Which airline is the most popular?

This presentation's objective is to display visualizations that support the conclusions drawn from an analysis of the "Data Expo 2009: Airline on Time Data."

Dataset Overview¶

A sample of 200,000 records from each year between 1987 and 2008 made up the gathered data set. I removed unused columns, deleted duplicates, and dropped null values during the wrangling process. Additionally, I added new columns (dummy variables) to facilitate exploration and performed transformations (merging, changing data types) on a few columns. The clean and dataset spans the years 1995 to 2008 and includes 11 columns and 2,730,250 values.

Flight information (counts)¶

  • Are there more flights on weekends?
  • How consistent are daily flight totals?

Weekly flights show a steady number of flights during weekdays (Monday-Friday) and a slight decrease in the number of flights on weekends.

Daily flights show that the last few days of the month see a sharp decline in flight demand.

Flight information (delays)¶

  • When is the best day of the week to fly and arrive on time?
  • What percentage of flights are late flights?

Weekday delays¶

The majority of on-time flight records occur on Tuesday. The most late arrivals/departures occur on Thursdays and Fridays.

Arrivals¶

78.7% of flights arrive on time, leaving 21.3% of arrival flights Late.

Carrier information¶

  • Which airline is best known for its short, medium, and long-distance flights?
  • Which airline is the most popular?

Popular carriers according to frequency:¶

9 Air (AQ), the carrier with the lowest frequency, is the least popular, and Southwest Airlines (WN), the carrier with the highest frequency, is the most popular.

Most popular carriers according to flight lengths are:¶

Short-haul flights under 600 miles are most frequently flown by Southwest Airlines (WN), while medium- (under 3000 Miles) and long-haul flights(greater than 3000 Miles) are most frequently flown by American Airlines (AA).

Interesting insight (i)¶

There was a significant correlation between distance and airtime. The longer the carrier is in the air, the further the destination is; airtime increases as distance increases.

Interesting insight (ii)¶

In comparison to on-time arrivals, late arrivals have a marginally higher airtime value. There are many potential causes for this, including the weather or technical difficulties. Airtime should therefore be taken into account when there are delays in arrival.
